Greenfish Labs — 2014–2017 · Lancaster, PA

When the tools didn’t exist, he built them.

When commercial VR took off in 2014, the production tools didn’t exist yet. No high-resolution 360° cameras you could buy. No real-time stitching software. No way to livestream VR content. No way to view VR on a phone. So Ben built them.

Ben was one of the first developers to acquire the original Oculus DK2 — the developer kit Oculus shipped before the consumer Rift — and started engineering production tooling around it from day one. Greenfish Labs ran on hardware and software he built from scratch:

Custom 360° stereoscopic cameras

Combined fisheye lenses with 4K cameras, capture cards, and the latest computer hardware. Scaled rigs up to 14 cameras recording simultaneously to capture true stereoscopic depth across a full sphere — pushing the resolution ceiling of what early headsets like the DK2 could display.

Real-time live-stitching workstations

Custom-built systems that combined synchronized multi-camera feeds into a single 360° stream as it captured.

Custom VR livestreaming software

No commercial pipeline existed for broadcasting live VR, so he wrote one. It powered the first nationally-broadcast mainstream-media VR livestreams.

Custom iOS mobile VR viewer app

One of the first apps that could display VR content on a phone — Greenfish built it because no other app on the App Store could. When Google Cardboard launched, the app supported it for large-scale audience reach.

Unity-based interactive VR software

Beyond film, Greenfish shipped interactive products like VR Golf Simulator and VR Build Space — using Unity to push VR into product demonstration and architectural visualization.

Validation

Nokia sent Greenfish pre-release OZO development cameras to field-test, then partnered with the studio on the first live VR broadcast of Pope Francis saying mass at World Youth Day 2016 in Kraków, Poland — alongside American Bible Society.

American Bible Society also brought the team into FOX and Dick Clark Productions for The Passion (2016) — case study, a 360° companion to the live broadcast. That work opened a multi-year DCP partnership: 2017 Billboard Music Awards in 360° (among the first nationally-broadcast mainstream-media VR livestreams — BTS won Top Social Artist that night, captured in 360°), Dick Clark’s New Year’s Rockin’ Eve, and more.

Greenfish did live VR sports too — the 2017 Phillies Opening Day Ceremony ran in 360°.
